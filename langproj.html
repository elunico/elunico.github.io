Creating Conlangs (short for constructed language) has been a hobby of mine for many years. I enjoy exploring features of natural languages and finding ways of incorporating them in new and interesting combinations. Conlangs can be created based on an existing lineage of languages such as the Romance languages. The Romance languages are numerous and varied and it can be a lot of fun trying to mix and match some of the less common processes that produce some of the rarer features. This is one of my favorite ways of conlanging. Conlangs can also be made from scratch with no influence (at least directly) from any existing languages. This involves creating the sound inventory, the way the sounds are realized, grammar, morphology and all the words in the language. It is considerably more work but very rewarding.

Many of the languages that I have created are at varying levels of completion. This is because, typically, if I find a language is not working in practice as well as it is in theory I will choose to change things up, but I usually leave the language where it is and start again on the same base but without whatever the problematic feature is. Furthermore, it is hard to say when any language is complete. Conlangs, like natural languages, can evolve either by natural spoken processes (a good example of this is El Glheth Talossa by R. Ben Madison) or it can "evolve" purposely by the creator defining evolutionary rules.

Llengua Provitana is a constructed Romance language and one of the most recent languages I created. It is based on the languages around Catalonia and has a heavy resemblence to Catalan. It differs in some key ways and includes some "regional variation" between two fictitious regions of east and west.

Provitan exists as an article on The Conlang Wiki where a description of the language can be found.

Lingua Lațiense

Lingua Lațiense is another constructed Romance language (I have something of a thing for Romance languages) that is a little older than Provitan. For simplicity, I will refer to the language as Latiense from now on. Latiense is fairly well developed but it is based on Romance languages from the east. Specifically those east of La Spezia–Rimini Line. There are notable contrasts, as observed by professional linguists, that differentiate languages north and west of this line (such as Spanish and French) from those south and east of this line (such as Italian or Romanian) although not all Romance languages fit so neatly in this paradigm.

Latiense does not currently exist anywhere on the Internet as Provitan does. This is due to it being a little older and not as well organized in its documentaion as Provitan.

Tilõ Niqacaw

FIXME (language sample, picture, something)

Tilõ Niqacaw is a conlang that is not based on any other language. That is not to say that it uses features that are unheard of in any language. In fact it uses sound and grammar features exclusively found in natural human languages. However, the choice of which features are used and their combinations as well as all the words are not directly taken from any existing natural language.

Tilo is the most developed a priori conlang that I have made and it is also the most recent. It is also one of my favorites as the philosophy and design behind it, to me, it the best and nicest looking. That being said, it is still fairly behind the development of the Romance languages. This is due to the sort of "template" that Romance languages provide. Creating a conlang of a Romance language can be quite restrictive, because there are many sources of features that can be drawn from. In the case of something like Tilo, everything has to be chosen and decided on, so its a much slower process.
